## Recruitment-Specific Frameworks

### **Competency-Based Assessment Framework**
**Based on:** McClelland (1973) - "Testing for Competence Rather Than for Intelligence"
**Extended by:** Spencer & Spencer (1993) - "Competence at Work"

**What to say:**
*"Our scoring dimensions align with competency-based assessment principles, originally developed by McClelland and later refined by Spencer and Spencer. This framework evaluates candidates based on measurable competencies rather than subjective impressions."*

---

### **Structured Interview Methodology**
**Based on:** Campion et al. (1997) - "The Structured Interview: A Review and Research Agenda"
**Key Principle:** Standardized, job-relevant criteria

**What to say:**
*"We apply structured evaluation principles similar to structured interview methodology, which research by Campion and colleagues has shown to be significantly more valid and reliable than unstructured approaches."*

---

## Company Reputation & Employer Branding

### **Employer Branding Theory**
**Developed by:** Ambler & Barrow (1996) - "The Employer Brand"
**Extended by:** Backhaus & Tikoo (2004) - "Conceptualizing and Researching Employer Branding"

**What to say:**
*"Our company tier system is informed by employer branding research, particularly the work of Ambler and Barrow, which demonstrates that employer reputation significantly influences candidate quality and hiring outcomes."*

---

### **Signaling Theory in Recruitment**
**Developed by:** Spence (1973) - "Job Market Signaling"
**Applied to recruitment by:** Rynes (1991) - "Recruitment, Job Choice, and Post-Hire Consequences"

**What to say:**
*"We incorporate signaling theory principles - candidates from prestigious companies send positive signals about their capabilities, as established in Spence's foundational work on job market signaling."*
